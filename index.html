Water Pollution

Water pollution refers to the contamination of water bodies such as rivers, lakes, oceans, groundwater, and streams by harmful substances, leading to a degradation of water quality...

Understanding its causes, effects, and solutions is crucial for preserving clean water for future generations.

Major Sources of Water Pollution

  • Industrial Waste: Toxic chemicals and pollutants discharged from factories.
  • Agricultural Runoff: Pesticides and fertilizers washed into water bodies.
  • Domestic Waste: Household sewage and plastic waste dumped into rivers and oceans.
  • Oil Spills: Large-scale contamination from oil tankers and drilling.
  • Deforestation: Leads to increased soil erosion and sedimentation in water sources.
