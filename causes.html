Causes of Water Pollution

Water pollution occurs due to multiple sources, both natural and human-made. Some major causes include:

  • Industrial Waste: Factories discharge harmful chemicals and toxic substances into water bodies, contaminating drinking and irrigation sources.
  • Agricultural Runoff: Excessive use of fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides seeps into groundwater and rivers, disrupting aquatic life.
  • Oil Spills: Accidental oil leaks from ships and refineries create devastating effects on marine ecosystems.
  • Plastic Waste: Non-biodegradable plastics clog rivers and oceans, harming marine species.
  • Sewage & Domestic Waste: Improper disposal of household waste and untreated sewage leads to the spread of diseases.

Understanding these causes is the first step toward finding solutions to water pollution.
